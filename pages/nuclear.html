Nuclear Decommissioning

Across the globe, a growing number of nuclear power plants are reaching the end of their operational lifespans and require urgent decontamination and decommissioning (D&D). In the United Kingdom, nuclear decommissioning is expected to continue for over a century, with total costs estimated at hundreds of billions of pounds, as reported by the Nuclear Decommissioning Authority (NDA). These operations involve extreme risk. Because radioactive and contaminated materials cannot be handled directly by humans.

Human access to nuclear sites is severely limited. Although engineers may enter controlled areas wearing protective suits, exposure time must be kept short to avoid excessive radiation. As a result, the nuclear industry has increasingly relied on teleoperated robotic systems to inspect, explore, and intervene in hazardous environments. Traditionally, these robots are controlled remotely via joysticks or sequential command inputs. However, long-distance communication introduces latency, signal degradation, and even complete loss of connectivityâ€”particularly in extreme, cluttered, or shielded environments.

At the same time, operators must build situational awareness using data from multiple onboard sensors, often with narrow fields of view. Interpreting camera feeds and sensor data to assess current conditions and anticipate future hazards places a heavy cognitive burden on operators. This challenge is amplified for less-experienced personnel, who may require extensive training to safely and effectively operate such systems. Improving situational awareness is therefore critical to mission success, especially when navigating complex, dynamic, and safety-critical environments.

Our Intelligent multi-robot solution addresses these challenges by introducing collaboration, resilience, and distributed intelligence into nuclear sites. Instead of relying on a single robot or centralised control, multiple robots work together, sharing information and supporting one another to maintain operational continuity, even when individual units face communication loss or sensor limitations. This cooperative approach significantly enhances robustness, a vital requirement for safety-critical industries such as nuclear decommissioning.

By fusing local sensor data from each robot into a distributed situational awareness framework, our system can rapidly and accurately explore unstructured environments without centralised infrastructure. This results in lower deployment costs, faster setup times, and greater operational flexibility. The outcome is a safer, more efficient, and more scalable robotic solution. It is designed to meet the long-term challenges of nuclear decommissioning and other hazardous industrial environments.
